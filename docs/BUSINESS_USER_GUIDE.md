# Business and User Guide

## 1. Business Goals
Jata aims to capture the mobile segment of the transit tracking user base. Native apps provide better retention rates, allow for push notifications (e.g., "Your usual bus is delayed"), and offer a smoother, more performant UI compared to web browsers.

## 2. Target Audience
Commuters who take public transit daily and prefer launching a dedicated app rather than navigating to a website. It is designed for users who value speed—opening the app to instantly see the map centered on their current location with live vehicle data already loaded.

## 3. User Manual

### Location Permissions
Upon first launch, Jata will request Location permissions. This is required to automatically jump the map to your current location and highlight nearby transit stops. If denied, you must manually search for your region.

### The Map Interface
- **Vehicles**: Represented by markers on the map. They animate smoothly between updates.
- **Stops**: Tapping a stop reveals the upcoming departures and estimated times of arrival (ETAs).

### Settings and Preferences
The app saves your settings locally on the device. Because it uses MMKV, these settings persist instantly and load without delay the next time you open the app.
